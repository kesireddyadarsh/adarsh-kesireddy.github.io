About Me

My journey into engineering and coding began with an innate curiosity to understand how things work. As I delved deeper into computational methods, I began applying artificial intelligence to real-world problems. My research led to two published papers on using neural networks for detecting the strength of steel through metallurgical image analysis. This project demonstrated how machine learning could revolutionize material science by predicting mechanical properties without destructive testing.

As an intern, I designed and optimized a welding solution for heavy pressure vessels using SolidWorks, applying contact mechanics and validating its effectiveness with Autodesk Algor. I collaborated with the manufacturing team to resolve design challenges through data-driven optimizations. Additionally, I converted 2D drawings into 3D models and digitalized the entire drawing set for enhanced accessibility and modifications.

After gaining experience in research and academia, I transitioned into industry, where I worked on enterprise software development and system optimization. I optimized SAP SD and MM modules by developing front-end solutions using a SaaS platform and programmed GuiXT using JavaScript to enhance data flow and system efficiency. I also managed software tool implementations from requirement gathering to deployment and support, ensuring smooth integration and usability.

Beyond enterprise software, I expanded into full-stack web development and industrial automation. I built a visitor check-in system with HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Java, and MySQL, incorporating automated notifications and database updates. I also developed a data pipeline for SPC and CPK, streamlining data collection, cleaning, and transformation using Smart Tree, C#, and Python. Additionally, I optimized GD&T analysis and customized measurement machine software, ensuring precision and efficiency using Python.
